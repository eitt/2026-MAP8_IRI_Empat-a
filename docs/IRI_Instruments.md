# Interpersonal Reactivity Index (IRI)  
## Conceptual and Linguistic Context — Spanish Versions

## 1. Conceptual Background

The **Interpersonal Reactivity Index (IRI)** is a multidimensional instrument designed to assess dispositional empathy through four theoretically distinct but related dimensions. Rather than treating empathy as a single affective response, the IRI distinguishes between **cognitive** and **affective** components, allowing for a more nuanced analysis of empathic functioning in social, moral, and decision-making contexts.

The four subscales are:

- **Perspective Taking (PT, Cognitive)**  
  The tendency to adopt the psychological viewpoint of others in everyday situations.

- **Fantasy (FS, Cognitive)**  
  The tendency to imaginatively identify with fictional characters in books, movies, or plays.

- **Empathic Concern (EC, Affective)**  
  Other-oriented feelings of compassion, sympathy, and concern for people in distress.

- **Personal Distress (PD, Affective)**  
  Self-oriented feelings of anxiety, discomfort, and unease when witnessing others’ suffering.

This multidimensional structure allows empathy to be analyzed as either adaptive or maladaptive depending on the configuration of its components (e.g., high Empathic Concern vs. high Personal Distress).

---

## 2. Spanish Adaptations Considered

For Spanish-speaking samples, multiple validated adaptations of the IRI are available. In this study, two recent and widely used Spanish versions were examined comparatively:

- **Arenas-Estévez et al. (2021)**  
- **Rangel et al. (2024)**  

Both adaptations:
- Preserve the original four-factor structure.
- Maintain the balance of cognitive and affective items.
- Include **attention checks** and a **commitment request** to ensure response quality.
- Clearly mark **reverse-coded items** (indicated with `*`).

Differences between the versions are primarily lexical and stylistic rather than conceptual.

## 5. Methodological Notes

- Reverse-coded items (`*`) must be recoded prior to computing subscale scores.
- Attention-check items should be used to exclude inattentive respondents.
- Both Spanish versions are psychometrically compatible with the original IRI.
- Lexical differences do not alter construct meaning but may affect respondent familiarity and perceived naturalness.

---

## 6. Use in the Present Study

The IRI is employed as a multidimensional measure of empathy, allowing separate analyses of cognitive and affective components. This approach enables the identification of distinct empathic profiles and supports advanced analytical strategies such as latent profiles, clustering, or structural modeling.
